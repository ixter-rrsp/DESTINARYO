Lakes and Mountains

PAGE 4

Mount Makiling – Laguna

A mystical dormant volcano covered in dense rainforest, Mt. Makiling is known for its myths, rich biodiversity, and challenging yet rewarding hiking trails.

Mount Pinatubo – Zambales, Tarlac & Pampanga

Famous for its cataclysmic 1991 eruption, this Zambales volcano now features a stunning crater lake. Trekking through its ash-laden landscapes offers a unique experience, with breathtaking views of the turquoise waters nestled within its rugged caldera.

Mount Pulag – Camiguin

The highest peak in Luzon, Mt. Pulag is famous for its sea of clouds, golden grasslands, and stunning sunrise views that make it a bucket-list destination for hikers.

Sumlang Lake- Albay

A serene and picturesque lake in Albay, Sumlang Lake offers a perfect view of Mayon Volcano, along with bamboo rafting, kayaking, and floating cottages.

Taal Volcano – Batangas

One of the world's smallest active volcanoes, located in Batangas. It features a crater lake within a larger lake, creating a unique landscape. Visitors can hike, take a boat ride, or enjoy breathtaking views from Tagaytay’s scenic spots.
