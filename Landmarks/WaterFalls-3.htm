WATERFALLS

PAGE 3

Lugnason Falls – Siquijor

A serene waterfall in Siquijor, offering a peaceful escape with its clear blue waters and lush surroundings. Known for its zodiac-themed pool, it's a hidden gem for those seeking a quiet, nature-filled retreat on the mystical island.

Maria Cristina Falls – Iligan City, Lanao del Norte

Known as the “Twin Falls” of the Philippines, Maria Cristina Falls is a spectacular 98-meter-high waterfall that serves as a major source of hydroelectric power in Mindanao.

Pagsanjan Falls – Laguna

One of the most famous waterfalls in the Philippines, Pagsanjan Falls is a stunning three-tiered cascade best known for its thrilling canoe ride adventure through lush gorges and rapid streams.

Pulangbato Falls – Valencia, Negros Oriental

Located in Valencia, Negros Oriental, this unique waterfall is named after its red-hued rocks, caused by natural sulfur deposits. Its warm waters, surrounded by volcanic landscapes, offer a therapeutic and picturesque retreat for adventure seekers.

Tangadan Falls – La Union

A two-tiered waterfall nestled in the mountains of San Gabriel, La Union, Tangadan Falls is a scenic paradise known for its rock pools, cliff-jumping spots, and serene natural beauty.
