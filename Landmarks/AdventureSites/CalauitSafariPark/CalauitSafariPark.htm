Calauit Safari Park

Calauit Island, Palawan

Calauit Safari Park offers a unique wildlife experience in the heart of Palawan, where visitors can encounter exotic animals roaming freely across the vast landscape. Spanning over 3,700 hectares, this natural sanctuary is home to African species like giraffes, zebras, and antelopes, alongside native Philippine wildlife such as Calamian deer and palawan bearded pigs. Originally established as a wildlife refuge in the 1970s, the park remains a living testament to conservation efforts and the harmonious coexistence of diverse species. Guests can join guided tours, feed the giraffes, and observe the animals up close, making for an immersive adventure surrounded by lush greenery and serene landscapes.

Beyond its wildlife encounters, Calauit Safari Park offers a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and adventurers alike. The unspoiled beauty of the island, with its rolling hills, mangrove forests, and pristine coastlines, creates an idyllic setting for exploration and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy a boat ride around the island’s waters, spot migratory birds, or simply soak in the views of the tranquil surroundings. The park’s commitment to conservation and educational tours also makes it an ideal destination for those looking to learn more about wildlife protection and the importance of preserving natural habitats.

Calauit Safari Park

A visit to Calauit Safari Park offers a one-of-a-kind experience, blending the thrill of a safari with the untouched beauty of Palawan’s landscapes. It’s a perfect escape for families, animal lovers, and photography enthusiasts eager to capture rare moments in an extraordinary setting. Whether you’re walking alongside giraffes, cruising through mangroves, or simply marveling at the wildlife, the park promises an unforgettable journey into the wild heart of the Philippines.
