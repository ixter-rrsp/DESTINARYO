Historical and Cultural Sites

PAGE 2

Calle Crisologo – Vigan

A well-preserved Spanish-era street in Vigan, featuring cobblestone paths, ancestral houses, and horse-drawn carriages. It offers visitors a nostalgic glimpse into the colonial past, making it a must-visit cultural heritage site in the Philippines.

Casa Manila – Manila

A Spanish colonial-style museum in Manila that showcases period furniture, artifacts, and architecture from the 19th century. It provides visitors with an immersive experience of the lavish lifestyle of wealthy Filipino families during Spanish rule.

Daraga Church – Albay

Also known as Nuestra Señora de la Porteria Church, Daraga Church is an 18th-century baroque church sitting atop a hill, offering a breathtaking panoramic view of Mayon Volcano.

Diplomat Hotel – Baguio

A historic and eerie abandoned hotel in Baguio, originally a monastery. It is now a famous haunted site, attracting visitors intrigued by its ghost stories, ruined facade, and panoramic views of the city from its hilltop location. El Fraile Island – Manila Bay

Also known as Fort Drum, this concrete battleship in Manila Bay served as a military fortress during World War II. Though abandoned, its unique history and structure continue to fascinate historians and adventure seekers.
