Historical and Cultural Sites

PAGE 4

Paoay Church – Ilocos Norte

A UNESCO-listed baroque church in Ilocos Norte, known for its massive buttresses and earthquake-resistant design. Built in the 18th century, it remains an architectural marvel and an important religious and historical landmark in the region.

Plaza Cuartel – Puerto Princesa

A former military barracks in Puerto Princesa with a dark history, where Japanese forces massacred prisoners of war during World War II. Today, it stands as a solemn memorial site honoring the fallen soldiers.

Quiapo Church – Manila

Home to the revered Black Nazarene statue, this historic church in Manila draws millions of devotees during its annual procession. It is a major religious landmark, known for its miraculous tales and vibrant spiritual atmosphere.

Rizal Park – Manila

A national landmark in Manila honoring Dr. José Rizal, the country’s national hero. This expansive park features monuments, gardens, and historical markers, making it a significant site for patriotism, leisure, and cultural reflection.

San Agustin Church – Manila

Also known as Fort Drum, this concrete battleship in Manila Bay served as a military fortress during World War II. Though abandoned, its unique history and structure continue to fascinate historians and adventure seekers.
