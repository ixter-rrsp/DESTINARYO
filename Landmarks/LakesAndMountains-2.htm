Lakes and Mountains

PAGE 2

Mayon Volcano – Albay

One of the world's most perfectly shaped volcanoes, Mayon in Albay is both stunning and active. Its picturesque cone dominates the landscape, offering adventure activities like trekking, ATV rides, and a breathtaking view from nearby ruins and farms.

Mount Apo – Davao del Sur

The "King of Philippine Peaks," Mt. Apo is the tallest mountain in the country and a challenging yet rewarding climb for adventure seekers.

Mount Arayat – Pampanga, Central Luzon

A dormant volcano in Pampanga, known for its mythical legends and scenic hiking trails. The summit rewards climbers with panoramic views of Central Luzon, while lush forests provide a habitat for diverse flora and fauna, including rare eagles.

Mount Balagbag- Rizal & Bulacan

A beginner-friendly hiking destination on the Rizal-Bulacan border, known for its open trails and breathtaking sunset views. With its grassy slopes and rock formations, it’s a favorite among trekkers looking for an easy climb near Metro Manila.

Mount Guiting-Guiting – Romblon

Known as one of the most difficult climbs in the Philippines, Mt. Guiting-Guiting is a rugged, knife-edged mountain that attracts experienced mountaineers seeking extreme adventure.
