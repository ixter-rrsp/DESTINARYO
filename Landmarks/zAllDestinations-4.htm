Philippines Tourist Spots

PAGE 4

Daraga Church – Albay

Also known as Nuestra Señora de la Porteria Church, Daraga Church is an 18th-century baroque church sitting atop a hill, offering a breathtaking panoramic view of Mayon Volcano.

Daranak Falls – Rizal

A serene and picturesque waterfall, Daranak Falls is a refreshing getaway just a few hours from Manila. It is a favorite spot for swimming, picnicking, and nature photography.

Dinosaur Island – Pampanga

A dinosaur-themed park in Pampanga, featuring life-sized, animatronic dinosaurs. Visitors can enjoy interactive exhibits, fossil displays, and a prehistoric jungle tour, making it an exciting attraction for kids and dinosaur lovers.

Diplomat Hotel – Baguio

A historic and eerie abandoned hotel in Baguio, originally a monastery. It is now a famous haunted site, attracting visitors intrigued by its ghost stories, ruined facade, and panoramic views of the city from its hilltop location. Dumaluan Beach – Bohol

A serene stretch of white sand in Bohol, known for its gentle waves, family-friendly resorts, and laid-back atmosphere. Ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water activities, it's a great alternative to the more crowded Alona Beach.

El Fraile Island – Manila Bay

Also known as Fort Drum, this concrete battleship in Manila Bay served as a military fortress during World War II. Though abandoned, its unique history and structure continue to fascinate historians and adventure seekers.

El Nido – Palawan

A tropical paradise in Palawan, renowned for its dramatic limestone formations, hidden lagoons, and pristine beaches. Island-hopping tours reveal breathtaking seascapes, while its vibrant marine life makes it a top destination for diving and snorkeling. El Nido is a breathtaking tropical paradise located at the northern tip of Palawan.

Enchanted Kingdom – Laguna

The most popular theme park in the Philippines, Enchanted Kingdom offers thrilling rides, magical experiences, and family-friendly entertainment.

Fort Santiago – Manila

A historic fortress in the heart of Manila, Fort Santiago is a symbol of the Philippines’ colonial past and resilience, serving as a defense structure, prison, and execution site during Spanish and Japanese rule.

Fort San Pedro – Cebu

The oldest and smallest Spanish fort in Cebu, built in the 16th century. It served as a military defense structure and now houses a museum with historical artifacts, offering visitors a glimpse into Cebu's colonial past.
