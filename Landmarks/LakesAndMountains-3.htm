Lakes and Mountains

PAGE 3

Mount Halcon – Oriental Mindoro

One of the toughest climbs in the Philippines, this Albay peak is known for its challenging trails, dense jungles, and stunning summit views. Rich in biodiversity, it’s home to rare species and offers an unforgettable trekking experience.

Mount Hamiguitan – Davao Oriental

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mt. Hamiguitan is home to the Philippines' famous pygmy forest, unique plant species, and endemic wildlife like the Philippine Eagle.

Mount Hibok-Hibok – Camiguin

A dormant volcano on Camiguin Island, Mt. Hibok-Hibok is an adventurous climb offering hot springs, lush forests, and a stunning panoramic view of Camiguin and its nearby islands.

Mount Kanlaon- Negros

The tallest peak in the Visayas, Mt. Kanlaon is an active volcano known for its crater lakes, sulfur vents, and challenging treks through dense forests and ridges.

Mount Kitanglad – Bukidnon

A majestic peak in Bukidnon, home to mossy forests, diverse wildlife, and the rare Philippine eagle. Hikers can enjoy a challenging ascent, rewarded with stunning sunrise views and a glimpse of the untouched beauty of Mindanao’s mountains.
